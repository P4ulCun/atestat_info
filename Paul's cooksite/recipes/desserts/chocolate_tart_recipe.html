Simple Chocolate Tart

This chic chocolate tart is a dessert to impress, and it’s much easier than it looks.

Ingredients

  • Butter - 120g

  • Sugar - 100g

  • Flour - 250g

  • Eggs - 2

  • Double cream - 300ml

 

  • Sugar - 2 tablespoons

  • Dark chocolate - 400g

  • Butter - 100g (for the ganache)

  • Milk - 100ml

  • Cocoa powder

Steps

  1. In a bowl combine the butter, sugar and salt and mix until pale and creamy.
  2. Add the flour and mix until it is incorporated, then do the same with the egg yolks.
  3. Using your hand, lightly knead the dough into a ball. Remove the dough from the bowl,
    press it into a 15cm disk, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap, and let it rest in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes.
  4. Pinch off pieces of dough and press them against the sides of the pan to reach less than a cm thickness.
    Using the heel of your hand, press the rest of the dough evenly into the bottom of the pan.
    Cover with plastic wrap and place in the freezer for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Place the chilled crust on a baking sheet and bake until dry and very lightly golden, about 20 minutes.

  6. Place the double cream, sugar and a tiny pinch of sea salt in a pan and bring to a boil.
  7. Break up the chocolate. As soon as the mixture has boiled, remove from the heat and add the butter and chocolate.
    Stir until it has completely melted. Allow the mixture to cool slightly, stirring in the cold milk until smooth and shiny.
  8. Scrape all the mixture into the cooked and cooled pastry shell with a spatula.
  9. Shake the tart to even it out and allow to cool for around 1 to 2 hours until it is at room temperature.
    Dust with the cocoa powder.

Enjoy!
